Searching online and talking to past cancer survivors gave me a rough estimate as to when but apparently everybody's hair grows at different rates.

Below is an approximate timeline as to how fast my hair grew back post-chemotherapy.

    3 weeks post-chemo: My hair was growing only on the side‚Äîkind of weird...
    6 weeks post-chemo: I have a visible hairline now from all my peach fuzz.
    10 weeks post-chemo: Enough hair to be messy, but my sides are still longer than the top.

In addition to products, your diet is extremely important so I ate many greens to give my body as many vitamins and nutrients it needed.

For the people who are going through it, I know it's tough. I used to wear a beanie or pull up my sweatshirt hoodie so no one could see my bare scalp, but I learned to get past that. Mirror were an obstacle for a while, but it gets better. This is a temporary state and hair always grows!

I sort of wanted to open up on how I figured out I have breast cancer because I know many of you reading probably have experienced something similar.

I'm standing in front of mirror, getting ready for bed. I take off my bra and notice something peculiar with my breasts. Feeling around, there was a very small, about marble sized, lump where there was creasing. I wasn't really anxious because breasts change with my cycle. I thought it was nothing though, so I decided to see if with time it went away. Why would I go to the doctor for such a small thing? After a week it was still there and it felt painful, so I decided to go see a doctor because by then it was larger than normal and it felt harder.

My mom's side has a history of having breast cancer, so obviously I was a bit nervous when thinking about the possibility of it being that. I wanted to get tested when I finished high school, but it's incredibly expensive at about $4,000. This BRCA gene test is a blood test that determines if there are mutations in my DNA that increase the risk of breast cancer. However, most breast cancer cases aren't hereditary‚Äîonly about 5% to 10%. But, the one that my mom and her mom had were BRCA 2 which is one of the most common inheredited breast cancer.
